Learning & Teaching

The lab's educational focus is on the transformation of AI pedagogy. Evidence-based frameworks and innovative pedagogical approaches are employed to prepare students for the rapidly evolving technological landscape.

SAGE Framework – Educator Guide GenAI 101 – Student Module

SAGE Framework

Structured AI-Guided Education is the lab’s flagship educational framework designed to develop AI orchestration competencies in students. SAGE has been validated across multiple Australian university campuses and has gained international adoption by institutions including the University of Warwick and the National University of Singapore. Further information, including implementation guidance for educators, is available on the dedicated framework page at SAGE Framework – Educator Guide.

The framework addresses critical competencies including prompt engineering, AI tool selection, critical evaluation of AI outputs, ethical considerations, and accessibility awareness in AI-enhanced workflows.

The SAGE 4-Step Cycle

At its core, the SAGE framework is built on a simple, repeatable cycle that moves students from passive AI consumption to critical AI orchestration. This process trains students to treat Generative AI as an assistive tool whose outputs must be rigorously evaluated, not as an authority to be copied.

The cycle involves four key stages:

  • Generate: Students use AI tools with structured prompts to create an initial output (e.g., a draft policy, code, or analysis).
  • Evaluate: Students compare the AI-generated output against domain-specific standards, research, and contextual requirements to identify gaps, errors, and biases.
  • Refine: Students modify, reject, or accept the AI's output, documenting their decisions with evidence-based justifications.
  • Reflect: Students conduct a metacognitive analysis of the process, identifying AI's limitations and the irreplaceable value of human domain expertise.
